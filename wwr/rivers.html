Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

Deep within the Frank Church Wilderness of No Return, the Salmon River, known as the "River of No Return," offers an unparalleled whitewater experience.

Towering ponderosa pines provide shade over the sandy riverbanks, while herds of elk, deer, and bighorn sheep roam the nearby hillsides.

With rapids ranging from Class I-IV, early summer offers fast and thrilling currents, while late summer provides calmer waters ideal for families.

Desolation and Gray Canyon, Utah

Flowing through the remote canyons of central Utah, the Green River offers a journey into some of the most rugged terrain in the West.

Surrounded by towering grey cliffs, this 84-mile adventure features stunning desert landscapes, ancient petroglyphs, and thrilling Class II/III rapids.

Recognized as a National Historic Landmark, Desolation Canyon provides a unique blend of history and adventure, making it an unforgettable experience.

Grand Canyon, Arizona

The Grand Canyon is the pinnacle of river adventures, offering breathtaking scenery and world-famous whitewater rapids.

Navigate through side canyons, towering cliffs, and ancient indigenous sites only accessible by river. The rapids range from mild stretches to Class X, the most intense on any rafting trip.

Whether you're an adrenaline-seeker or a nature enthusiast, the Grand Canyon delivers an unparalleled whitewater rafting journey.
